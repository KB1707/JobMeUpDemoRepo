Educational Requirements

Bachelor's Degree in Nursing (BSN)

Duration: Typically 4 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Nursing (BSN) is the foundational degree for a career in pediatric nursing. It provides comprehensive training in nursing theory, pediatric care, and clinical skills.

Registered Nurse (RN) Licensure

Duration: Varies (typically requires passing the NCLEX-RN exam)

After completing a BSN program, aspiring pediatric nurses must obtain licensure as a Registered Nurse (RN) by passing the NCLEX-RN exam. Licensure requirements vary by state.

Master of Science in Nursing (MSN) with Pediatric Specialty

Duration: 2-3 years

A Master of Science in Nursing (MSN) with a pediatric specialty provides advanced education and training in pediatric nursing practice, leadership, and research.
