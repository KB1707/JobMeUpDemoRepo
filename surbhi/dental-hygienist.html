Notable Experts

Alfred Fones

Pioneer of Dental Hygiene

Alfred C. Fones is considered the father of dental hygiene. He established the first dental hygiene school in the world in 1913 in Bridgeport, Connecticut.

Irene Newman

Advocate for Oral Health

Irene Newman was instrumental in establishing the first dental hygiene program at the University of Michigan in 1917. She played a key role in advancing the dental hygiene profession.

Esther Wilkins

Author and Educator in Dental Hygiene

Esther Wilkins is renowned for her contributions to dental hygiene education and authored the widely used textbook "Clinical Practice of the Dental Hygienist," which has been instrumental in shaping the profession.

Educational Requirements

Associate Degree in Dental Hygiene

Duration: Typically 2-3 years

An Associate Degree in Dental Hygiene provides foundational education in dental science, anatomy, oral health, and clinical practice necessary to become a licensed dental hygienist.

Bachelor's Degree in Dental Hygiene (Optional)

Duration: Additional 2 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Dental Hygiene offers advanced coursework and clinical experiences, providing opportunities for specialization and career advancement in the field.

State Licensure

Duration: Varies by state

Obtaining state licensure is essential for practicing as a dental hygienist. Requirements typically include passing written and clinical examinations after completing an accredited dental hygiene program.
