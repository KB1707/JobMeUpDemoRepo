Educational Requirements

Bachelor's Degree in Pharmacy (B.Pharm)

Duration: Typically 4 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Pharmacy (B.Pharm) is the foundational degree for a pharmacist. It provides comprehensive training in pharmaceutical science, pharmacology, and pharmacy practice.

Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D)

Duration: 4 years (after B.Pharm)

The Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D) is the professional degree required to become a licensed pharmacist in many countries. It focuses on clinical pharmacy practice, patient care, and pharmaceutical care.

Pharmacy Residency

Duration: 1-2 years (optional)

A pharmacy residency is a postgraduate training program that provides pharmacists with additional experience in a specific area of pharmacy practice, such as community pharmacy, hospital pharmacy, or clinical pharmacy.
