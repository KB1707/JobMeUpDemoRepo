Educational Requirements

Bachelor's Degree in Occupational Therapy

Duration: Typically 4 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Occupational Therapy provides foundational knowledge in human anatomy, physiology, and occupational therapy principles. Students also gain hands-on experience through fieldwork.

Master's Degree in Occupational Therapy (MSOT or MOT)

Duration: 2-3 years

A Master's Degree in Occupational Therapy is the minimum requirement for entry-level practice as an occupational therapist. It includes coursework in therapeutic techniques, assessment, and intervention planning.

Clinical Fieldwork and Professional Certification

Duration: Varies

After completing a master's program, occupational therapists must complete fieldwork under the supervision of licensed practitioners. They must also pass the National Board for Certification in Occupational Therapy (NBCOT) exam to become certified.
