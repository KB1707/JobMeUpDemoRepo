Educational Requirements

Associate Degree in Radiologic Technology

Duration: Typically 2 years

An Associate Degree in Radiologic Technology provides comprehensive training in radiography techniques, patient care, and radiation safety, preparing students for entry-level positions as radiologic technologists.

Bachelor's Degree in Radiologic Sciences (Optional)

Duration: Additional 2 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Radiologic Sciences offers advanced coursework in radiography, specialized imaging modalities, and healthcare management, providing opportunities for career advancement and specialization.

Radiologic Technologist Certification

Duration: Varies by certification

Obtaining certification from organizations like the American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) is essential for practicing as a radiologic technologist. Certification typically requires passing a standardized examination after completing an accredited radiologic technology program.

Radiologic Technology Resources

The American Registry of Radiologic Technologists (ARRT) is the primary credentialing organization for radiologic technologists in the United States. It offers certification and registration programs, as well as continuing education resources.
