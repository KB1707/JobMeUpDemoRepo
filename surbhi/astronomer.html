Educational Requirements

Bachelor's Degree in Astronomy, Physics, or related field

Duration: Typically 4 years

A Bachelor's Degree in Astronomy or Physics provides a solid foundation in mathematics, physics, and observational techniques necessary for a career in astronomy.

Master's Degree in Astronomy or Astrophysics

Duration: 2-3 years

A Master's Degree offers advanced coursework and research opportunities in specialized areas such as astrophysical instrumentation, cosmology, or planetary science.

Ph.D. in Astronomy or Astrophysics

Duration: 4-6 years (after Bachelor's degree)

A Ph.D. program provides intensive research training and culminates in a doctoral dissertation, preparing astronomers for careers in academia, research, or observatories.
